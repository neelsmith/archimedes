1 Introduction
==============

The purpose of this document is to make clear which texts appear on
which folios of the Archimedes Palimpsest, and, where possible, to
indicate the folio sequence of each text. This document also lists all
the Archimedes Palimpsest folios included in the data set, organized
by author and work, undertext folio, and prayer book folio.

1.1 2011 amendments
-------------------

Since 2008, scholars have learned more about the non-Archimedes
content of the palimpsest.  New texts have been identified, and
misattributions of some of the texts have been discovered.  This has
led to changes to the abbreviations for the textual content of the
underlying manuscripts.  These changes are used in the Archimedes
Palimpsest print catalog (Reviel Netz, William Noel, Natalie
Tchernetska, Nigel Wilson, eds. *The Archimedes Palimpsest*. 2
vols. Cambridge, UK: Cambridge University Press, 2011).

Although there are new content designations for the folios, the names
of the image files and supporting metadata have not been changed in
this data set.  Data added to the set since 2011 conforms with
information in the print publication.

The final section of this document describes the nomenclature used in
the 2011 print publication and provides a concordance between the 2008
and 2011 folio designations.



2 The Folios in the data set
============================

This data set consists of evidence of texts that were palimpsested in
the thirteenth century to make a prayer book, or Euchologion. This
prayer book is commonly known as the Archimedes Palimpsest.  In total
there is evidence for 177 palimpsest folios, and the evidence is
listed below.

 1. Digital Images of the Archimedes Palimpsest, now in private
    hands. The Archimedes Palimpsest currently consists of 174
    parchment folios, and a single gathering of eight paper folios,
    added at a later date.  The parchment leaves are foliated 1-177 in
    a modern hand.  Folios 104, 123, and 170 are currently missing.
    Only the parchment folios are palimpsest, and images of all of the
    extant folios are in the current data set. The paper folios, which
    have neither foliation nor pagination, have not been been included
    in this data set.
 
 2. Digital images of analog images of the manuscript, taken at
    various times in the twentieth century.  This includes images that
    were taken in Constantinople in 1906 at the request of Johan
    Ludwig Heiberg, the originals of which are in the Royal Library
    Copenhagen, Ms. Phot. 38. The Heiberg photographs include folios
    of the palimpsest that have subsequently been excised from the
    manuscript and are currently missing. These are included in the
    data set foliation.  These are fol. 123, recto and verso, and
    fol. 170 recto.
 
 3. Digital Images of a single leaf of the Archimedes Palimpsest,
    excised before it was foliated, and now Cambridge University
    Library Ms. 1879.23. These will be included in the candidate
    release of this data set.
 
 4. The only evidence extant for folios 104 and 170v of the manuscript
    is transcription of the undertext that was seen on them by Johan
    Ludwig Heiberg between 1906 and 1911. Included in the final
    release of this data set will be new transcriptions based upon
    Heiberg’s, by Netz and Wilson.

3 The texts in the data set
===========================

The Euchologion known as the Archimedes Palimpsest contains several
palimpsest codices.

 1. It contains seven treatises by Archimedes, and it forms Codex C in
    Heiberg’s 1910-15 edition of Archimedes; these seven treatises are
    Equilibrium of Planes, Spiral Lines, The Measurement of the
    Circle, Sphere and Cylinder, On Floating Bodies, The Method of
    Mechanical Theorems, and the Stomachion.
 
 2. It contains fragments of two speeches by Hyperides, Against
    Diondas and Against Timandros.
 
 3. It contains fragments of a philosophical text which has been
    provisionally identified as Alexander of Aphrodisias, Commentary
    on Aristotle’s categories.
 
 4. It contains fragments from a Life of St. Pantoleon.
 
 5. It contains fragments from a Menaion.
 
 6. It contains fragments from an unidentified text (Unknown A).
 
 7. It contains fragments from a second, distinct unidentified text
    (Unknown B).

4 The foliation of the data set
===============================

The Euchologion was foliated in the second half of the Nineteenth
Century in red Arabic numerals, at the top right of each folio.  This
data set presents images of the Euchologion in a disbound state.
Hence, when the Euchologion folios are not singletons, they are
presented as conjoint folios.  Thus it is usually the case that a
single image of the Euchologion will reference two folio numbers.

There is no evidence in the manuscript for the separate foliation of
the palimpsested texts.  Foliations for each of the palimpsested
undertexts have been assigned for the purposes of this data set. The
texts are identified by abbreviations. The abbreviations used are:

 * Alex -- Alexander of Aphrodisias' Commentary on Aristotle's
   Categories
 * Arch -- Archimedes
 * Hype -- Hyperides
 * Mena -- Menaion
 * Pant -- The Life of St. Pantoleon
 * UnkA -- Unknown text A
 * UnkB -- Unknown text B

In each case the folios are presented in the data set with the
undertext reading from the top to the bottom of the image.

8 Concordance of 2008 and 2011 folio designations
=================================================

This section provides a folio-by-folio cross-walk between 2008 and
2011 undertext nomenclature. As noted above, the print publication
uses a set of abbreviations that reflect an updated understanding of
the textual content of the palimpsest undertexts.

The publication uses this set of abbreviations:

 * ``Arch`` -- Archimedes
 * ``Hype`` -- Hyperides
 * ``Comm`` -- Philosophical commentary (formerly ``Alex``)
 * ``Mena`` -- Menaion
 * ``Hagi`` -- Hagiographical text (formerly ``Pant`` and ``UnkA``)
 * ``UnkY`` -- Unknown text 'Y' (folios of the former ``UnkB``)
 * ``UnkZ`` -- Unknown text 'Z' (a folio of the former ``UnkB``)

Changes:

Hagi 
   The hagiographical text was previously identified as the Life of
   St. Pantoleon and given the abbreviation ``Pant``; also, the folios
   of the text previously called Unknown A were discovered to be a
   part of this document.

Comm 
   The philosophical commentary was thought to be the work of
   Alexander of Aphrodisias and was given the abbreviation ``Alex``.
   This attribution was found to be uncertain, and so the new
   designation ``Comm``, for commentary, is now used.

UnkY, UnkZ
   The text called Unknown B was found to contain not one unidentified
   manuscripts, but two, now designated ``UnkY`` and ``UnkZ``

The following tables give folio-by-folio crosswalks between the 2008
and 2011 designations of the undertexts.
